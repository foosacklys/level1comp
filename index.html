Level 1 Competency Model

Coach, First and Foremost
  • Observes, Then Steps In With Purpose
    • Watches for growth moments—not just mistakes.
    • Coaches in the moment when team members struggle.
  • Gives Frequent, Real-Time Feedback
    • Offers immediate, specific praise and guidance.
    "Nice job catching back up—appreciate the hustle."
  • Teaches, Not Just Tells
    • Explains the 'why' behind the task.
      "We do it this way to improve flow and avoid getting behind."
  • Holds and Reinforces Standards
    • Doesn’t let things slide just because it’s busy.
    • Holds team accountable with care and clarity.
  • Builds Confidence
    • Recognizes wins, big and small.
    • Encourages growth beyond comfort zones.
Good Communicator
  • Sets Clear Expectations
    • Outlines goals and metrics before and during the shift.
    "We’re aiming for 3-minute ticket times AND great quality."
  • Stays Calm and Direct Under Pressure
    • Gives clear direction without panic.
    "We’re backed up—can you hop to drinks please?"
  • Delivers Respectful Feedback
    • Chooses constructive phrasing.
    Not: "You’re too slow." Instead: "Let me show you how to move tickets faster without losing accuracy."
Self-Awareness
  • Acknowledges their own impact.
"My tone was off earlier. I’m sorry about that."
  • Adjusts behavior mid-shift.
“I’ve been a little negative—I need to change that."
  • Owns mistakes without defensiveness.
  • Controls emotions and resets attitude when needed.
Emotional Intelligence
  • Coaches rather than reacts harshly to mistakes.
"Frustrating, I get it. Let’s not let it get us down."
  • Checks in with team members emotionally.
"You seem off today—everything okay?"
  • Rephrases when miscommunication happens.
"That didn’t land how I meant it—let me rephrase.”
  • Does not let emotions create a false narrative of people or get in the way of staying positive and delivering feedback.
  • Works on themselves daily instead of comparing to others.
Self-Discipline
  • Stays late to finish the job when needed.
  • Leads with consistent energy despite outside stress.
  • Takes and applies tough feedback.
"Thanks for the notes—I’ll communicate better during shift change."
Integrity
  • Follows through on commitments.
  • Maintains consistency whether or not leadership is present.
  • Holds themselves and the team accountable to systems and standards.
  • Speaks up when something is wrong.
Positive Attitude
  • Brings steady, positive energy.
  • Recognizes team wins in real-time.
  • Focuses on solutions.
"Let’s fix it," not "Whose fault is it?"
Resilience
  • Stays calm and focused when shifts get chaotic.
  • Bounces back from a tough shift. Doesn’t let it get them down.
  • Doesn’t let personal stress impact the shift.
  • Trust’s how they are being developed even when hitting temporary setbacks.
“I don’t see it fully yet, but I trust the process.”
Growth Mindset
  • Asks questions when unsure.
"Can you walk me through that?"
  • Accepts and applies feedback. Doesn’t get down on themselves.
  • Seeks new challenges and opportunities to grow.
Servant Leadership
  • Takes on hard tasks as well. Doesn’t delegate them all.
  • Regularly checks in with team members.
"What do you need from me?"
  • Stays and finishes the job until the team is set up for success.
Teamwork
  • Jumps in to help without being asked.
  • Maintains positive energy and encouragement.
  • Focuses on solving issues, not placing blame.
Customer Experience & Satisfaction focus
  • Engages customers with a friendly and welcoming attitude.
  • Practices the BLAST method for handling complaints.
  • Identifies opportunities to exceed guest expectations.
  • Knows when and how to escalate service issues.
  • Rallies the team around the customer experience.
  • Exhibit a passion for service
Shift Management
  • Plans and Organizes Efficiently
    • Ready and prepared ahead of shift.
    • Understands team strengths and opportunities.
    • Anticipates and plans for business spikes.
  • Punctual, Prepared, and Energized
    • Arrives on time, prepared with a plan.
    • Maintains professional appearance and presence.
  • Maintains Focus and Urgency
    • Has a “let’s go!” mindset that carries through prep and ahead of peak hours.
    “Hey y’all, let’s lock in —  we are staying ready for lunch rush!”
    • Stays engaged even when slow.
    • Adjusts team roles in real time when needed.
    • Moves with urgency but calmly.
  • Ensures All Positions Run Smoothly
    • Moves around and checks each station regularly.
    • Steps in or assigns help where needed.
    • Supports front-of-house and kitchen equally.
  • Relieves Bottlenecks Quickly
    • Monitors ticket times and makes adjustments early.
    "Let’s move Alex to tickets. I’ll run food for a few minutes."
    • Fixes issues first, then follows up for long-term improvement.
  • Food Quality & Presentation
    • Stops sub-par food before it leaves the line.
    "This toast is too old—let’s drop a fresh one please."
    • Conducts regular visual checks to ensure quality food and presentation.
    • Enforces FIFO consistently.
    • Ensures proper pacing for hot fresh food.
  • Service Flow & Efficiency
    • Monitors the whole restaurant, not just assembly..
    • Tracks ticket times and identifies slow points.
    • Adjusts positions, floaters, and tasks to maintain pace.
Technical Skills & Compliance
  • Operational Mastery
    • Proficient in FOH & BOH roles and able to train others.
    • Ensures compliance with food safety, sanitation, and labor laws.
    • Oversees inventory and receiving processes.
    • Coaches team members on equipment maintenance and safety protocols.
    • Uses operational tools like Schoox for training and performance tracking.
Opening and Closing Responsibilities
  • Start and End-of-Day Excellence
    • Executes all opening and closing procedures efficiently.
    • Maintains high cleanliness and organization standards.
    • Ensures all early and late customers are treated well.
    • Handles POS troubleshooting and cash handling securely.
    • Ensures the restaurant is open on time and ready to serve.
    • Leads end-of-day walkthroughs and ensures all team members clock out together.
Captainships & Development Opportunities
  • FOH Trainer – Guides front-of-house team members.
  • BOH Trainer – Coaches back-of-house staff on execution.
  • Sauce Master – Ensures consistency in sauces and flavor.
  • Food Quality Captain – Maintains high food standards.
